## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 32 days to enforcement (August 1, 2026)**: DROP platform (launched January 1, 2026) allows California residents to submit a single deletion request to all 575+ registered data brokers for free. August 1 enforcement means brokers must check DROP every 45 days, delete matching records within 90 days, and face $200/request/day penalties for non-compliance. This is the biggest structural event facing paid removal services this year.
- **Senate investigation**: 2026 report found major brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) placed code on opt-out pages to block search engine indexing — inadvertently strengthening the value proposition of paid services that navigate these friction points programmatically.
- **Market size signal**: An estimated 26 million people are expected to adopt a data broker removal service in 2026, per industry projections cited in current review content. With approximately 238 million US adults, penetration remains very early-stage — significant headroom for all players.
- **Federal legislation watch**: Identity theft losses linked to data broker exposures estimated at $21 billion annually (2026 congressional findings). Growing bipartisan attention may push a federal data broker bill before year-end — would reshape the DROP-vs.-commercial-service calculus nationally.
