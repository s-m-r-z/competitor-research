# Competitor Insights — 2026-06-16

## DeleteMe
- Pricing remains $129/year (individual), $229 for a 2-year plan; no monthly billing option available.
- Covers approximately 86–101 data broker sites, scanning every 90 days — well below newer competitors on both breadth and frequency.
- Consumer Reports rates DeleteMe a "mid-level performer," behind Optery and EasyOptOuts which cleared 65%+ of found profiles.
- Facing increasing price/value pressure: GhostVault (emerging) offers 500+ broker coverage at $39/year; Incogni covers 180+ for ~$78/year.
- Brand positioning leans on longevity and human-assisted removals for complex cases, but reviewers broadly note limited scope as a growing liability.

## Regulatory & Policy
- **Senate JEC Report (February 2026):** Senator Hassan's investigation found that five major data brokers — Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights, and Findem — placed "no-index" code on opt-out pages to hide them from search engines. Four of the five removed the code after Congressional pressure. **Findem has not complied and has not responded**, raising serious concerns about its opt-out practices.
- **FTC enforcement activity:** FTC finalized action against Illuminate Education on June 5, 2026, for failing to secure student personal data — broader data handling accountability signal for the industry.
- **TAKE IT DOWN Act (enforced from May 19, 2026):** FTC is enforcing new requirements for platforms to remove nonconsensual intimate images within 48 hours — expands the regulatory scope data removal services may eventually face.

## Emerging / Noteworthy
- **California DROP Platform** (launched January 1, 2026): California Privacy Protection Agency's free Delete Request and Opt-Out Platform now covers 543 registered CA data brokers with a single submission. Starting August 1, 2026, brokers must comply within 90 days. This is a structural headwind for paid services targeting California consumers.
- **California Privacy Agency "Data Broker Strike Force":** Active enforcement crackdown underway — raises compliance risk for data brokers and may accelerate market consolidation.
- **GhostVault** (emerging): Positioning on price/value — 500+ broker coverage at $39/year, drawing direct comparisons to DeleteMe in 2026 reviews.
- **EasyOptOuts**: Rated alongside Optery as a top performer in Consumer Reports evaluation (65%+ profile removal rate); gaining traction in review comparisons.

## Market Summary
The data broker opt-out space is intensifying on two fronts: Optery is pulling ahead on awards, enterprise credibility, and coverage breadth, while regulatory action (California's DROP platform and new Strike Force enforcement) is compressing the value proposition of paid consumer-tier services. OneRep's non-public broker expansion and Incogni's aggressive June discounting signal the mid-market is heating up, putting price-to-coverage pressure squarely on DeleteMe's legacy positioning.
