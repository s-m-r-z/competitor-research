# Competitor Insights — 2026-06-22

## DeleteMe
- Continues to operate as a premium manual-removal service; privacy experts handle removals that automation misses.
- A 2024 Consumer Reports investigation rated DeleteMe a "mid-level performer," trailing Optery and EasyOptOuts in removal success rates (below 65% of profiles removed).
- No new product announcements or pricing changes detected in the last 48 hours.

## Incogni (Surfshark)
- Expanded custom removal database to **3,000+ unique domains** as of March 2026 — the largest broker coverage in the category.
- Launched new **"Protect" plan** bundling data removal with identity theft protection and Coveron insurance.
- Running a promotional **55% discount** (code: CYBERNEWS55) for June 2026; annual Standard plan now ~$7.19/month.
- Multiple independent reviews (TechRadar, AllAboutCookies, CyberNews) continue to rank Incogni as the #1 data removal service.

## Optery
- Won **three 2026 Cybersecurity Excellence Awards**: Attack Surface Management, Anti-Phishing, and Human Risk Management.
- Won **Globee® Award Best of Category: Social Engineering** (April 2026).
- Published **2026 Enterprise Social Engineering Survey Report** (May 12, 2026) based on 421 cybersecurity leaders; report positions data broker exposure as the top attacker intelligence vector — reinforcing Optery's enterprise pitch.
- Ranked **#12 Fastest-Growing Private Company** on Inc. Regionals: Pacific list.
- PCMag Editors' Choice for the **5th consecutive year** (2022–2026).
- Coverage now at **640+ automated removals** and **1,000+ total sites** including custom requests.

## OneRep
- **Expanded to 550+ non-public data brokers** (announced May 13, 2026) — targets behind-the-scenes data circulation, not just public people-search profiles.
- Added **custom removal requests** for sites outside standard coverage and **data breach monitoring** with real-time alerts.
- Reputation headwind persists: Krebs on Security investigation linking CEO to data broker companies previously caused Mozilla to drop OneRep from Mozilla Monitor. No new developments on this front.

## Privacy Bee
- No new product or pricing announcements detected in the last 48 hours.
- Continues to differentiate through aggressive tactics: legal threats, regulatory complaints, and multi-channel pressure when brokers ignore standard opt-outs.
- Positioned as a premium, family-oriented "set-and-forget" service with tracker blocking and breach alerts.

## Kanary
- No new product announcements detected in the last 48 hours.
- Competitive differentiator remains **continuous monitoring** (vs. weekly/monthly scans at most rivals), enabling faster re-listing detection.
- Coverage: 300+ brokers.

## Removaly
- **SHUT DOWN** — Removaly has ceased operations. This is the most significant competitive development this cycle.
- Industry sites (Increditools, others) are already publishing "Removaly alternatives" roundups, with Incogni named the top replacement.
- Opens an opportunity to capture displaced Removaly subscribers.

## Emerging / Regulatory Developments
- **California DROP Platform**: Launched January 1, 2026; allows free one-stop deletion requests to all 543+ registered California data brokers. Data brokers must process deletions by **August 1, 2026** — a potential headwind for paid removal services among California consumers.
- **US Senate investigation**: Found several major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) using code to block opt-out pages from search engine indexing — likely to generate regulatory and media attention.
- **DataPurge**: Emerging tool generating legally-backed deletion requests citing CCPA, GDPR, and 17+ state laws in one click — a low-cost DIY alternative gaining attention.
- **Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List (BADBOOL)**: Updated May 31, 2026 with new opt-out links — signals continued community-driven competition to paid services.

## Market Summary
The data broker opt-out space is consolidating around Incogni and Optery at the top (with Incogni's new "Protect" bundle and Optery's enterprise momentum), while Removaly's shutdown removes a mid-tier competitor and leaves an immediate acquisition opportunity for the remaining services. California's DROP platform launching full enforcement in August 2026 is the most significant macro threat to the paid-service business model and warrants close monitoring.
