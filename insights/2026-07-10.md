# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-10

## DeleteMe
- Continues to hold brand recognition as an established player covering 750+ data brokers, re-checking every 90 days
- Rated a "mid-level performer" in Consumer Reports evaluation; Optery and EasyOptOuts outperformed DeleteMe on removal rates
- Pricing remains at ~$10.75/month (annual plan); maintains an A+ BBB rating and human-assisted service model
- No new product announcements detected in the past 48 hours

## Privacy Bee
- Restructured pricing into three tiers: Essentials (people-search sites only), Pro (broader broker coverage), and Signature (priority support + hands-on remediation); monthly billing and multi-year discounts now available
- Claims 1,000+ data broker contacts, with weekly dashboard updates — a differentiated cadence vs. the industry-standard monthly
- Added breach monitoring alerts and tracker blocking as bundled features
- Recent April 2026 test recorded 125 removals requested and 169 closed over ~2 months

## Kanary
- Major pivot: Kanary has shifted to a mobile-first app called **Kanary Copilot**, available on iOS only (Android waitlist open); the web app remains active for existing subscribers only
- Covers 300+ data brokers with a hybrid automated/manual approach claiming 95–100% removal rates by day 90
- Premium tier priced at $9.99/month; also offers $14.99–$16.99/month (annually) with family add-ons
- No new announcements in the past 48 hours; the Copilot pivot appears to have been the primary 2026 product move

## Emerging & Market Signals
- **California DROP** (Delete Request and Opt-Out Platform): Launched January 1, 2026 by CalPrivacy. A free, government-run tool allowing CA residents to send a single deletion request to 600+ registered data brokers. **August 1, 2026** is the enforcement deadline — data brokers must begin processing requests within 90 days. This could reduce the perceived need for paid services among California residents.
- **Connecticut Data Broker Law**: Signed May 27, 2026 — requires data broker registration, new consumer rights, and restrictions on surveillance pricing. The regulatory wave is expanding beyond California.
- **Industry consolidation**: Publicis acquired LiveRamp in 2026; Omnicom acquired Acxiom's parent in 2025 — major ad conglomerates absorbing data sources, increasing the upstream complexity that opt-out services must navigate.
- **OfflistMe** is appearing in comparison roundups as an emerging low-cost alternative worth monitoring.

## Market Summary
The competitive landscape is being reshaped from two directions simultaneously: Incogni's Deloitte-verified audit and aggressive pricing is pulling market share at the budget end, while California's free DROP platform (with mandatory enforcement kicking in August 1, 2026) creates a long-term threat to subscription-tier demand among CA-based customers. Optery's active Summer promotion and search-engine removal expansion signal an effort to widen the moat before the regulatory tailwind normalizes baseline expectations.
