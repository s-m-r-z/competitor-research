## Regulatory & Market Context
- **California DROP enforcement: T-10 days (August 1, 2026)** — 600+ registered data brokers must begin processing 260,000+ queued consumer deletion requests; $200/consumer/day penalty for non-compliance; theoretical maximum liability at 260,000 consumers is $1.5 billion per missed cycle
- **Compliance requirements from August 1**: data brokers must retrieve DROP requests at least every 45 days, match using standardized identifiers, delete personal data absent a statutory exception, and complete determinations within 90 days of retrieval
- **California Privacy Protection Agency Strike Force** active: enforcement posture is proactive, not reactive; non-compliance risk is real and immediate
- **Connecticut data broker law** enacted in 2026 modeled on California's Delete Act, expanding the regulatory map beyond California and signaling multi-state momentum
- **19 state comprehensive privacy laws** in effect as of mid-2026; data broker obligations are a consistent thread across state regimes
- **Consumer adoption**: 26 million consumers projected to start using a data broker removal service in 2026; category awareness at an all-time high entering the DROP enforcement window
- **DIY pressure**: California DROP (free, state-operated, covers 575+ registered CA brokers) may dampen low-end commercial demand but validates the category and creates premium upsell opportunities around non-CA coverage, speed, and monitoring continuity
