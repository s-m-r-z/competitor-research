# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-04

## DeleteMe
- Coverage expanded to 950+ data broker sites; pricing remains $129/year (1 person) to $499/2 years (family), or $6.97–$34.40/month depending on plan tier
- Reviews consistently updated through May 2026 confirm service delivers "real but modest" results — genuine reduction in spam calls and Google search exposure over time
- Positioned as most reliable choice for users wanting detailed reporting and the longest track record (founded 2010); no new product announcements detected in the past 48 hours

## Privacy Bee
- Holds coverage of 1,000+ data broker sites including non-public databases and lead generation platforms; mid-February 2026 update confirmed this figure
- Three annual tiers: Essentials ($96/yr), Pro ($197/yr), Signature ($804/yr — executive tier with dedicated support); no monthly billing option
- Differentiates via bundled tools: tracker-blocking browser extension, private search engine, Gmail inbox tracker scanning, and Google search result removal requests
- No new press releases or product announcements detected in the 48-hour window

## Incogni (Surfshark)
- Most active competitor this cycle: as of June 2026, expanded Exposure Scanner to cover *unlimited* custom domains, not just its standard 420+ broker list
- Recently introduced a mobile app, added 24/7 support options, identity theft protection to the Protection Plan, and eliminated the power-of-attorney requirement for new users
- Independently audited by Deloitte, a differentiator it is actively marketing; ranked #1 in multiple July 2026 comparison roundups
- Current pricing: Standard $7.99/mo ($95.88/yr), Unlimited $14.99/mo, Family $15.99/mo

## OneRep
- **Significant May 2026 expansion**: added coverage of 550+ *non-public* data brokers (behind-the-scenes data circulation), custom removal requests, and data breach monitoring alerts
- Prior standard coverage was 230 public people-search sites; the non-public expansion is a notable competitive move
- Ongoing controversy: founder retains ownership stake in Nuwber, a data broker OneRep claims to remove users from — this remains a reputational risk and is cited in competitor reviews

## Optery
- **May 12, 2026**: Released *The Data Behind the Deception* — 2026 Enterprise Social Engineering Survey Report based on 421 cybersecurity leader responses; signals a continued push into the B2B/enterprise segment
- Running a Summer 2026 sale: 20% off with code `SUMMER2026`
- SOC 2 Type II certification completed; PCMag Editors' Choice for the fifth consecutive year (2022–2026)
- Awarded Cyber Defense Magazine's Market Disruptor Award and Cybersecurity Excellence Awards in Human Risk Management and Anti-Phishing in 2026
- Introduced optional AI-powered removals via OpenAI integration
- Pricing: Core $39/yr, Extended $149/yr, Ultimate $249/yr — among the most affordable entry points in the market

## Kanary
- Transitioned to a mobile-first product (Kanary Copilot app); currently iOS-only with an Android waitlist — a significant UX bet that may alienate web-first users
- Covers 300–600 data broker sites at $9.99/month; 2024 Consumer Reports investigation rated it a "mid-level performer"
- Recent testing shows slow removal pace (~6 profiles deleted in first two weeks), making it harder to defend against faster automated competitors
- No new announcements detected in the 48-hour window

## Removaly
- Effectively defunct since early 2022; website shows a vague team message with no active service
- DuckDuckGo acquired the underlying opt-out list but reduced coverage compared to the original
- Still appearing in "best of" roundups for historical reference only; not a current competitive threat

## Market Summary
California's government-run DROP platform (Delete Request and Opt-Out Platform) begins mandatory data broker processing on August 1, 2026 — a potential demand headwind for paid services as consumers gain a free state-backed alternative, though its scope is limited to California-registered brokers. Meanwhile, a 2026 U.S. Senate investigation exposed major data brokers blocking search engine indexing of their opt-out pages, reinforcing the value proposition for automated removal services and likely generating favorable press for the category in the near term.
