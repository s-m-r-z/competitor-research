## Regulatory & Policy
- **California DROP (August 1, 2026 deadline — 44 days out):** All registered data brokers (~543) must process deletion requests via the state portal within 90 days. This is the single largest near-term market disruptor; free statewide opt-out tool competes directly with consumer-tier paid services in California. Watch for competitor messaging responding to this deadline.
- **Vermont H 211 (signed or pending Governor Scott):** Final bill passed both chambers May 28–29, 2026 with universal deletion portal provision stripped; only funds a $50K feasibility study. Far weaker than California's mechanism.
- **US House SECURE Data Act (proposed May 12, 2026):** Would establish a new federal privacy framework for data brokers. Early stage — legislative momentum to track.
- **Senate investigation finding:** Multiple major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) confirmed to be blocking search engine indexing of their opt-out pages — directly undermining consumer ability to opt out manually and strengthening the paid-service value proposition.
