## Removaly / DuckDuckGo Privacy Pro
- Removaly remains shut down. DuckDuckGo Privacy Pro's Personal Information Removal (powered by the 2023 Removaly acquisition) is the successor product.
- DuckDuckGo PIR covers **50+ data broker sites** — far fewer than any standalone removal service tracked. All processing happens on-device rather than via remote servers, which is a unique privacy architecture but limits scalability.
- Broker list last updated April 17, 2026 — no update in approximately 10 weeks. Bundled with Privacy Pro's VPN and identity theft remediation at $10/month.
- "Removaly alternatives" search traffic continues to funnel to Incogni and Optery via roundup content; DuckDuckGo's low broker count means it rarely appears in head-to-head comparison lists.

## Emerging Competitors — Watch Signal
- **EasyOptOuts**: At $19.99/year, it is the lowest-priced paid service in the category and was rated a top performer by Consumer Reports (65%+ removal rate). Appearing more frequently in budget-focused roundups as an alternative to Incogni.
- **PrivacyOn**: A new entrant now ranking as "best overall 2026" on the PrivacyOn.com domain's own roundup content — likely affiliate-driven positioning, but the brand is gaining SERP presence. Coverage: 100+ brokers with continuous re-monitoring, dark web monitoring, and family plans (up to 5 people).
- **GhostMyData**: Positioning as the top EasyOptOuts alternative; free exposure scan with live compliance report is the lead UX hook. Gaining presence in comparison roundups alongside established players.
- **CrabClear**: Targeting EasyOptOuts migration traffic; claims 1,500+ data broker removals and EU law backing. Very early-stage but worth tracking as a coverage-breadth challenger.

## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 34 days to enforcement**: August 1, 2026 enforcement deadline is 34 days out. DROP has surpassed 242,000 consumer sign-ups and 575 registered data broker registrations as of the CPPA's February 2026 board meeting — both significantly above initial forecasts. The $200/request/day penalty structure is now generating significant compliance anxiety among lead-generation data brokers specifically (per industry trade commentary).
- **Congressional findings**: A 2026 investigation cited identity theft losses from data broker breaches at $21 billion annually; separate findings revealed that major brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) placed robots.txt-style blocking on their opt-out pages to prevent search engine indexing of those pages — a finding that strengthens the value proposition for paid removal services that handle these friction points programmatically.
- **CPPA enforcement posture**: Active enforcement docket includes Tractor Supply ($1.35M, Jan 2026), Honda ($632.5K), Todd Snyder ($345K), with smaller fines for data broker registration failures. Signals continued escalation ahead of the August deadline.

## Market Summary
No major product launches, pricing announcements, or feature releases were detected from tracked competitors in the last 24–48 hours — the category remains in a pre-enforcement holding pattern ahead of the August 1 California DROP deadline (34 days out). The most notable tactical signal today is Incogni's active 60% off promotion (the deepest discount seen this week, sharper than Optery's ongoing 20% Summer Sale), while Optery's Authorized Agent positioning under the California Delete Act stands out as the most strategically differentiated move in the market. OneRep's reputational damage from the Mozilla partnership termination continues to compound, and the emergence of PrivacyOn, GhostMyData, and CrabClear signals the low and mid-market is beginning to attract new entrants ahead of what could be a regulatory tailwind moment for the entire category.
