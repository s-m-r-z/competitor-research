# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-19

## DeleteMe
- No new product or pricing announcements in the past 24–48 hours; individual annual plan at $10.75/month ($129/year) unchanged; a Premium tier at $180/year adds bi-monthly reports and 60 custom removal requests/year
- Coverage count now publicly cited at 950+ sites, though third-party Consumer Reports testing continues to rank DeleteMe as a "mid-level performer" on actual removal success rates vs. Optery and EasyOptOuts
- California DROP enforcement now T-13 days (August 1); still no observed urgency-driven marketing campaign — a persistent gap given DeleteMe's brand recognition and ability to reach a mainstream consumer audience
- No free trial remains a conversion liability as competitors with free tiers and free scans actively capitalize on DROP-driven consumer discovery

## Privacy Bee
- No new announcements in the past 24–48 hours
- Three-tier pricing (Essentials ~$8/month, Pro ~$18/month, Signature ~$67/month, all billed annually) confirmed current; Signature tier's human remediation and priority support remain the highest-touch offering in the premium segment
- Weekly dashboard refresh cadence (vs. competitors' monthly or quarterly cycles) is the strongest differentiation claim for active users — not yet surfaced prominently in paid acquisition messaging
- High-risk user vertical (domestic abuse survivors, stalking victims, executives, law enforcement) continues to occupy an uncontested niche; no competitor has launched targeted messaging or features for this cohort

## Kanary
- No new product announcements in the past 24–48 hours; Kanary Copilot app confirmed live on both iOS and Android following Android gap closure flagged in prior cycle
- Cumulative removals now publicly cited at 12,887+ unique websites — a trust-building metric that few competitors surface with comparable specificity
- 300+ broker coverage with transparent privacy policy and 14-day free trial; $9.99/month positions it as mid-market; family add-on and transparency reports remain differentiators vs. comparably priced alternatives
- Settings Audit & Fix (social/Google account privacy remediation) remains a category-unique feature with no visible competitive response

## Incogni
- No new announcements; European expansion now covers all EU countries plus UK, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Isle of Man, and Liechtenstein — broadest geographic reach in the category and a strategic moat against UK/EU-based competitors
- Deloitte-verified removal confirmation from 420+ brokers and 245M+ total removal requests processed serve as high-credibility third-party proof points underused in comparison-shopping contexts
- Surfshark One+ and NordVPN ecosystem bundling continues to drive low-cost user acquisition without equivalent paid CAC; no competitor has replicated this bundle-distribution model
- Standard plan at $7.99/month (annual) remains among the most competitive entry-price points for fully automated coverage

## Optery

- Open-source data broker directory (640+ brokers, opt-out links, contact emails, company profiles) launched on GitHub in April 2026 and continues to generate organic developer and journalist coverage; positions Optery as a transparency-forward player vs. the black-box approaches of peers
- SUMMER2026 promo code (20% off annual plans) remains active; Core $3.99/month, Extended $10.99/month, Ultimate $24.99/month tiers unaffected
- CNET Best Overall 2026 and PCMag Editors' Choice (5th consecutive year) designations continue to anchor top-of-funnel search authority
- Y Combinator backing and award haul (Cybersecurity Excellence Awards in 3 categories, Globee® Cybersecurity Best of Category) continue to add institutional credibility

## OneRep

## Regulatory & Market Context
- **California DROP enforcement: T-13 days (August 1, 2026)** — All 575+ registered data brokers must begin processing deletion requests via the DROP portal starting August 1; $200/consumer/day fines apply for non-compliance; 260,000+ consumer pre-submitted requests are queued; this is the single largest near-term demand catalyst in the category
- **US Senate investigation**: Senators found that several major data brokers — including Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, and 6sense Insights — placed code on their opt-out pages blocking search engine indexing, effectively hiding opt-out mechanisms from consumers; this finding strengthens the regulatory and reputational pressure on the broker ecosystem and implicitly validates managed removal services
- **Industry adoption milestone**: An estimated 26 million consumers are expected to start using a data broker removal service in 2026, driven by rising privacy awareness and regulatory momentum — the largest single-year adoption surge in the category's history
- **Publicis/LiveRamp consolidation**: Publicis's 2026 acquisition of LiveRamp continues to consolidate consumer data infrastructure into fewer ad conglomerates, narrowing DIY opt-out efficacy and strengthening the managed removal value proposition
- **Help Net Security (July 10)**: Data broker deletion response rates remain at 26–38%; most brokers do not inform consumers of outcomes — a persistent trust and efficacy gap that managed services can actively market against

## Market Summary
The competitive landscape on July 19 is defined by two divergent plays: Optery's new Map Blurring feature and its open-source directory are driving a transparency and feature differentiation narrative, while OneRep's May 2026 non-public broker expansion gives it the deepest coverage story in the category. With California DROP enforcement now 13 days away and 26 million consumers expected to enter the market in 2026, the window for urgency-driven acquisition messaging is at its peak — Optery and Incogni remain best positioned to capture this demand spike.
