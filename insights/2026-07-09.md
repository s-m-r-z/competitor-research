# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-09

## DeleteMe
- Coverage now cited at 750+ data brokers (up from the 700+ figure in earlier 2026 reviews), with 100M+ opt-outs processed since 2011 — the highest milestone count of any competitor.
- Continues to rely on a hybrid model: automated opt-outs for the 100–125 highest-traffic brokers, manual specialist review for the remainder; quarterly re-check cadence remains slower than Incogni's continuous model.
- Pricing ranges $6.97–$34.40/month depending on plan length and number of people covered; no structural pricing changes detected today.

## Privacy Bee
- Three-tier structure (Essentials / Pro / Signature) now confirmed with monthly billing, multi-year discounts, and family coverage options — a notable structural shift from the previous flat annual price.
- Officially claims 1,000+ data broker and people-finder site coverage, plus custom requests across 150,000+ non-standard sites — the widest stated coverage in the market by a significant margin.
- Weekly privacy audit (risk-ranking exposures by severity) continues to differentiate Privacy Bee from monthly-cadence competitors; remains one of the pricier options with no promotional pricing observed today.

## Incogni (Surfshark)
- **New product tier confirmed**: The Protection Plan at $223.99/year ($18.67/month) now bundles Incogni with Coveron (formerly NordProtect), adding $1M identity theft insurance, dark web monitoring, and credit monitoring — a direct move into Aura/Identity Guard territory.
- Post-4th-of-July promotional pricing (58% off) is winding down; standard annual pricing returning.
- Expansion Scanner now covers any website containing user data — not just the 420+ registered broker list — following the June 2026 product update; mobile app (iOS and Android) is live.
- 24/7 support options and family plans (up to 5 members) added in June 2026 round-up.

## OneRep
- Updated pricing structure confirmed: Individual $8.33/mo (annual), Family $15.00/mo for up to 6 people (annual), Individual Pro $15.95/mo, Family Pro $29.95/mo — all-in family pricing is notably competitive vs. Privacy Bee and DeleteMe family tiers.
- Trust concerns persist: founder Dmitri Shelest's ownership stake in Nuwber (a data broker OneRep claims to protect against) continues to surface in reviews; Mozilla's Firefox partnership termination in late 2025 continues to weigh on brand perception.
- No new partnerships, features, or product announcements detected today.

## Optery
- Summer 2026 sale still active: promo code SUMMER2026 gives 20% off; tiers run from Core ($3.99/mo, 120+ sites) to Ultimate ($24.99/mo, 640+ automated sites + 955+ total with unlimited custom requests).
- Independent testing in 2026 reports a 90% removal confirmation rate over four months — the highest verified removal rate cited in current benchmarks.
- PCMag Editors' Choice for fifth consecutive year (2022–2026) and Globee Cybersecurity award (2026) provide ongoing third-party validation for enterprise and B2B positioning.
- SOC 2 Type II certification (AICPA) achieved — a compliance differentiator for business-tier customers.

## Kanary
- Kanary Copilot (mobile-first iOS app) now the primary product for new customers; Android waitlist open but no launch date announced — a meaningful conversion gap given Android's ~45% US market share.
- Covers 300+ data brokers; $16.99/month or $179.88/year (annual plan); 14-day free trial available.
- Transparency reporting (showing exactly which brokers were contacted and when) highlighted as a distinguishing feature vs. competitors with opaque removal dashboards.
- Removal pace remains the primary criticism in independent reviews; slow processing vs. Incogni and Optery.

## Removaly
- No activity. Service ceased independent operations in early 2022; subsequently connected to DuckDuckGo. Not a competitive factor in 2026.

## Emerging & Notable

- **PrivacyOn**: Gaining analyst attention in mid-2026 as a full-stack alternative — claims 100+ broker removals, continuous re-monitoring, dark web monitoring, and family plans up to 5 people at a competitive price point. Worth tracking.
- **EasyOptOuts**: Continues to appear alongside Optery in independent benchmark results as a value-tier top performer; low-cost positioning attracting price-sensitive segments.
- **CrabClear**: EU-focused data removal service gaining traction with GDPR-mandate-driven demand; not a direct US competitor but signals international market expansion opportunity.

## Market — Regulatory Developments

- **California DROP enforcement countdown (23 days):** August 1, 2026 is now 23 days away. A July 8 TechTimes article confirmed that 600+ registered data brokers must begin processing 260,000+ queued consumer deletion requests or face $200-per-day fines per unfulfilled request — a compliance wave with nine-figure fine exposure at stake. This remains the most disruptive near-term macro force for subscription-based services targeting California residents.
- **Impact on removal services:** The free DROP platform neutralizes the California data-broker-opt-out pitch for price-sensitive consumers; services must emphasize non-public brokers (TransUnion, Experian, LexisNexis-type), breach monitoring, and nationwide coverage outside CA to retain pricing power.

## Market Summary
The competitive landscape on July 9, 2026 is defined by two converging pressures: Incogni's push upmarket into identity theft protection (via the Coveron bundle) is intensifying at the premium end, while California's free DROP platform — 23 days from enforcement — is compressing willingness to pay at the consumer end. Services with differentiated coverage of non-public brokers, strong removal verification rates (Optery's 90%), or bundled security features (Incogni's $1M insurance) are best positioned to hold pricing power through the August regulatory inflection point.
