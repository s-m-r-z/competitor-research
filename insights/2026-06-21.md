## Regulatory & Policy
- **California DROP — 41 days to enforcement (August 1, 2026):** Data brokers must begin processing requests in 45-day retrieval cycles; non-compliance penalties of $200 per request per day. CalPrivacy Strike Force enforcement (latest: Datamasters/Rickenbacker Data LLC fined $45,000) continues ahead of the deadline. The compliance crunch will likely drive renewed earned media for paid services emphasizing monitoring and re-listing protection beyond the one-time DROP request.
- **US Senate JEC Report (Feb 2026 — ongoing coverage):** Major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) confirmed to have used `noindex` on opt-out pages to suppress discoverability. Findem cited as sole holdout still not compliant with Senate removal requests. Ongoing press reinforces the "DIY opt-out is deliberately obstructed" narrative that benefits paid services.
- **US House SECURE Data Act (proposed May 12, 2026):** Federal data broker registration/deletion framework; no new legislative movement this week.
