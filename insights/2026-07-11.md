## Emerging & Market Signals
- **California DROP enforcement — T-minus 21 days**: August 1, 2026 is when the $200/day-per-request fine regime activates for the 600+ registered data brokers. 260,000+ consumers have already submitted requests via DROP. This is the single largest near-term structural event facing the paid removal market.
- **DataPurge** is appearing in 2026 roundups as an emerging competitor; differentiator is legally-backed deletion requests citing CCPA, GDPR, and 17+ state privacy laws sent with one click — watch for traction.
- **Connecticut Data Broker Law** (signed May 27, 2026) adds a second state-level enforcement regime beyond California; multi-state regulatory pressure is building.
- **Ad industry consolidation**: Publicis/LiveRamp and Omnicom/Acxiom integrations are concentrating upstream data supply, potentially increasing the breadth of opt-out work required — a tailwind for services with broad or custom-removal capability.

## Market Summary
The next three weeks are the most consequential short-term window in the market: California's DROP enforcement kicks in August 1, creating both a consumer awareness spike (potential top-of-funnel lift) and a "why pay when the state does it free" narrative that could blunt CA-based conversion. Incogni's Exposure Scanner expansion and aggressive 55% discount, combined with Optery's home-map blurring feature, signal that leading services are racing to add defensible value beyond standard people-search removals before that regulatory tailwind normalizes baseline expectations.
