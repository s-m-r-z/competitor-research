## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 26 days to enforcement (August 1, 2026)**: DROP platform (live since January 1, 2026) allows California residents to file a single deletion request to all 575+ registered data brokers for free. Starting August 1, data brokers must check DROP at least every 45 days, delete matching records within 90 days, and face $200/request/day penalties for non-compliance. All tracked services are increasingly positioning themselves as complements to (not replacements for) DROP.
- **Senate investigation findings**: 2026 congressional report confirmed major brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) actively placed code on opt-out pages to block search engine indexing — reinforcing the value proposition of paid services that navigate opt-out friction programmatically.
- **Market size**: ~26 million US consumers expected to adopt a data removal service in 2026; penetration of ~238 million US adults remains early-stage, suggesting significant runway for all players.
- **Federal legislation**: Identity theft losses tied to data broker exposures estimated at $21 billion/year (2026 congressional findings); bipartisan momentum building for a federal data broker bill that could reshape the DROP-vs.-commercial-service calculus nationwide.
