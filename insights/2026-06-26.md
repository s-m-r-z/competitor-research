## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 35 days to enforcement**: The August 1, 2026 deadline for data brokers to begin processing DROP deletion requests is now 35 days out. The platform has 575+ registered data brokers and 215,000+ consumer sign-ups. Non-compliance exposure: $200/request/day on top of $200/day registration fines.
- **CPPA Data Broker Strike Force — enforcement escalating**: CalPrivacy's Data Broker Enforcement Strike Force has now issued at least two public enforcement actions: Datamasters (Texas-based) fined $45,000 for failing to register; ROR Partners LLC (Nevada-based) fined $56,600 for the same violation. Both were ordered to cease selling California resident data. Signals the CPPA is actively pursuing non-registrants ahead of August 1 — could drive more brokers to comply with DROP requests and reduce the friction that paid removal services navigate on behalf of customers.
- **Consumer Reports legacy audit**: The 2024 Consumer Reports finding — that DeleteMe, IDX, and Kanary are "mid-level performers" while Optery and EasyOptOuts top the rankings with 65%+ removal rates — continues to recirculate in June 2026 comparison content and materially shapes buyer perception.
