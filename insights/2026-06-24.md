## Emerging / Regulatory Developments
- **California DROP — 38 days to enforcement**: August 1, 2026 deadline for data brokers to begin processing DROP deletion requests (every 45 days, within 90-day completion window) is now very close. Platform has surpassed **242,000 consumer sign-ups and 575 data broker registrations** in ~8 weeks since launch — faster adoption than projected. Penalty exposure: $200/request/day for non-compliance on top of $200/day registration fines. Perkins Coie analysis estimates aggregate industry exposure at $1.5B. Paid removal services should expect a narrative challenge: California consumers may question the ROI of paid services if DROP effectively delivers free bulk deletion.
- **US Senate broker investigation ongoing**: Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, and 6sense Insights still under scrutiny for using code to block opt-out pages from search engine indexing. No new Senate action detected today, but media pickup continues and an FTC referral remains possible in H2 2026.
- **Congressional cost study**: February 2026 estimate of $21B annual consumer harm from data broker breaches continues to circulate in federal privacy legislation discussions — background signal for potential federal preemption bills later this year.
- **DataPurge (emerging DIY tool)**: Gaining SEO traction with free legally-backed deletion letters citing CCPA, GDPR, and 17+ state laws. No new feature releases detected today; remains a longer-term free-alternative threat to low-end paid tiers.
