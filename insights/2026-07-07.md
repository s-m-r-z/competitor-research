## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 25 days to enforcement (August 1, 2026)**: DROP platform milestone: **242,000 consumer sign-ups and 575 registered data brokers** in its first eight weeks (live since January 1, 2026). August 1 compliance deadline requires data brokers to retrieve DROP requests every ≤45 days, delete matching records within 90 days, and face **$200/request/day** penalties for non-compliance. All tracked services are visibly repositioning DROP as a complement ("we do what DROP can't") rather than ignoring it.
- **Ad-tech consolidation**: Publicis Groupe completed its acquisition of LiveRamp in 2026; Omnicom acquired Acxiom's parent (Interpublic) in 2025. Both transactions concentrate large volumes of consumer data into fewer hands — potentially increasing the number of high-value brokers that opt-out services must cover, and raising the barrier to comprehensive removal.
- **Federal legislation momentum**: $21 billion/year in identity theft losses tied to data broker exposures (2026 congressional findings); bipartisan support for a federal data broker bill continues to build. Passage could either validate the industry (compliance complexity increases) or commoditize it (mandatory free opt-outs reduce the paid-service value proposition).
