## Regulatory / Industry Developments
- **California DROP — 31 days to enforcement (August 1, 2026)**: DROP (launched January 1) allows California residents to submit one free deletion request to all 575+ registered data brokers. August 1 triggers mandatory compliance: brokers must check DROP every 45 days, honor requests within 90 days, and face $200/request/day penalties for violations. Largest structural event facing the commercial removal market in 2026.
- **California Privacy Agency Data Broker Strike Force**: CPPA launched a dedicated enforcement unit targeting unregistered brokers and DROP non-compliance — enforcement posture meaningfully more aggressive than 2025.
- **US Senate investigation**: Found that major brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) placed code on opt-out pages blocking search engine indexing — strengthens the case for programmatic removal services that navigate friction-by-design.
- **FTC PADFAA enforcement**: Federal Trade Commission continuing enforcement of the Protecting Americans' Data from Foreign Adversaries Act (2024), focused on brokers selling data on US service members. Elevates the national security angle of data broker legislation.
- **Federal legislation watch**: Congressional findings cite $21B annually in identity theft losses linked to data broker exposure. Bipartisan momentum may push a federal data broker bill before year-end — would reshape the DROP-vs.-commercial-service calculus beyond California.
- **AI reconstruction risk**: Noted in multiple 2026 roundups that AI systems can increasingly reassemble personal information from disparate public sources even after removal — an emerging threat to the efficacy promise of all removal services.
