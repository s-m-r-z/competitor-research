# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-08

## DeleteMe
- Continues to operate as the most established data broker removal service, re-checking opt-outs every 90 days at ~$10.75/month.
- Per Consumer Reports, rated a "mid-level performer" behind Optery and EasyOptOuts, which removed 65%+ of profiles found.
- Faces increasing headwinds from California's free DROP platform, which now covers 600+ data brokers at no cost to CA residents.

## Privacy Bee
- Launched a three-tier pricing structure (Essentials, Pro, Signature) with monthly billing, multi-year discounts, and multi-person family coverage.
- Essentials targets people-search sites only; Pro adds hundreds of additional data broker sources; Signature adds priority and hands-on remediation.
- Weekly dashboard updates position it as more real-time than most competitors that scan monthly; remains one of the pricier options in the market.

## Incogni (Surfshark)
- Running a 4th of July promotional deal: 58% off annual plans with code COOKIE — strong acquisition push around the holiday weekend.
- Expanded Exposure Scanner now covers any website containing user data (not just registered brokers), plus added a mobile app and 24/7 support channels.
- Coverage independently verified by Deloitte; reports 420+ brokers, 245M+ removal requests processed to date.
- Available standalone or bundled in Surfshark One+ starting at $3.99/month — the bundle angle remains a key differentiator vs. pure-play competitors.

## OneRep
- In May 2026, expanded coverage to 550+ non-public data brokers (TransUnion, Experian, LexisNexis-type) with custom removal requests and breach monitoring added to Pro plans.
- Released YourControl, a free open-source Chrome extension to help users manage data held by major non-public brokers — a rare freemium/community-driven move.
- Trust overhang persists: Mozilla ended its Firefox bundling partnership in late 2025 following KrebsOnSecurity reporting that CEO Dimitri Shelest co-founded and maintains ownership in Nuwber, an active people-search data broker.

## Optery
- Won Best of Category — Social Engineering, 2026 Globee® Awards for Cybersecurity.
- Earned PCMag Editors' Choice for the fifth consecutive year (2022–2026).
- Running a Summer 2026 sale with promo code SUMMER2026 for 20% off; Ultimate plan now covers 640+ automated sites plus 955+ total with unlimited custom removal requests at $24.99/month.
- Completed AICPA SOC 2, Type II certification — a compliance milestone that strengthens enterprise and B2B positioning.
- Consistent top performer in independent audits alongside EasyOptOuts.

## Kanary
- Pivoted product strategy: Kanary Copilot, a mobile-first app (iOS only, Android waitlist open), has replaced the web product for new customers — existing web subscribers retain access.
- This pivot to mobile may limit near-term growth given Android users are excluded; represents a notable strategic bet vs. browser-based competitors.
- Covers 300+ data brokers; premium tier at $9.99/month; 14-day free trial still available.
- Data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (SSL); MFA supported.

## Removaly
- No meaningful activity detected. The service appears to have ceased reliable operations around early 2022; no new 2026 product or company news found.
- Effectively no longer a competitive factor in the market.

## Market — Regulatory & Emerging Developments
- **California DROP enforcement deadline (August 1, 2026):** Data brokers must now process deletion requests within 90 days, with $200/day/consumer fines for non-compliance. Over 300,000 Californians have already submitted DROP requests, which data brokers must process by September 15, 2026 — potential $60M+ in fines at stake. This is the most significant near-term threat to subscription-based removal services targeting CA residents.
- **FTC enforcement signal:** The FTC's $930,000 settlement with Cox Media Group signals continued federal attention on data brokers, likely reinforcing demand for removal services among privacy-conscious consumers nationwide.
- **EasyOptOuts** is emerging as a top-rated low-cost alternative; repeatedly cited alongside Optery in independent benchmark results.

## Market Summary
The data broker opt-out market is bifurcating: premium services (Privacy Bee, DeleteMe, OneRep Pro) are adding depth and bundle features to justify higher price points, while value-tier services (Incogni, Optery Core) are competing aggressively on price and audit credibility. California's DROP platform is the most disruptive macro force — its August 2026 enforcement deadline will compress consumer willingness to pay for CA-focused removal coverage, shifting competitive emphasis toward non-public brokers, nationwide coverage, and breach monitoring as key differentiators.
