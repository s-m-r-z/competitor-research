## Emerging / Noteworthy
- **EasyOptOuts:** Continues to share top-performer status with Optery in Consumer Reports-derived content (65%+ removal rates); budget-friendly positioning and strong DIY-assist narrative. No new announcements.
- **California DROP (state-backed free tool):** Live since January 2026; covers 543 registered data brokers with single-click opt-out. Compliance deadline for data brokers to begin processing requests: **August 1, 2026 — now 42 days away.** No paid consumer service has yet issued public messaging addressing this free alternative. The deadline is the next major market inflection point.

## Regulatory & Policy
- **California DROP (August 1, 2026):** Data brokers must begin processing single-click deletion requests within 45-day cycles. CalPrivacy has not issued new enforcement guidance since January 2026; the August 1 deadline is 42 days out and is the category's most significant near-term market event.
- **CalPrivacy Strike Force — ongoing:** Datamasters (Rickenbacher Data LLC) fined $45,000 for failure to register under the Delete Act. Enforcement expected to intensify ahead of August 1. The Strike Force's activity implicitly strengthens the paid-service narrative around complexity and compliance burden.
- **US Senate JEC Report (Feb 27, 2026):** Major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) found to have placed "noindex" code on opt-out pages to block search engine discovery. Findem remains the lone holdout — the only named broker that has not removed the noindex code following Senate requests. Ongoing media coverage reinforces the "DIY opt-out is deliberately hard" narrative that benefits paid services.
- **US House SECURE Data Act (proposed May 12, 2026):** Federal data broker registration/deletion framework bill; no new movement. Still in early legislative stage.
- **Data broker industry size:** ~$278 billion market, per current industry estimates — context for why regulatory pressure is intensifying.
