# Competitor Insights — 2026-06-17

## DeleteMe
- Current pricing: $103.20/year ($8.60/month, 1-year plan) or $167.20/year ($6.97/month, 2-year plan); 2-person plan at $206.40/year and 4-person family plan at $412.80/year.
- Active promotional discount: 40% off in June 2026 — signals competitive pricing pressure, particularly against Incogni's 55–60% discounting campaigns.
- Coverage claims vary significantly across review sources (86–950+ sites cited), suggesting DeleteMe may be inflating "reviewed" broker counts vs. verified active removals — a trust/messaging risk as competitors publish harder evidence (Optery screenshots, Incogni Deloitte audit).
- Still relying on quarterly rescans; no announced frequency upgrades despite competitors moving to continuous or weekly monitoring.
- No new product announcements or feature launches found in the last 48 hours.

## Privacy Bee
- No new product or pricing announcements in the last 48 hours.
- Service covers 1,000+ data brokers with weekly dashboard refresh — remains the highest monitoring frequency among standard-tier services.
- Premium positioning intact: still the priciest option in the market, justified primarily for high-risk use cases (executives, domestic abuse survivors, public figures).
- Notable differentiator: multi-channel enforcement escalation (legal threats, regulatory complaints) when brokers resist standard opt-outs — no competitor publicly claims this approach.

## Incogni
- Family plan pricing confirmed: $14.39/month (annual) for up to 5 members on Standard; $22.99/month (annual) for Family Unlimited with custom removals for all 5 users — competitive value vs. Privacy Bee for multi-person coverage.
- Active June 2026 discount campaigns: 55–60% off promotional codes confirmed active across coupon aggregators; referral program offers 58% off to referred users on annual plans.
- Coverage: 420+ data brokers on Standard plan; 2,420+ with custom requests on Unlimited plan.
- Deloitte-audited removal practices remain a unique third-party trust signal in the market.
- No new product announcements in the last 48 hours beyond ongoing promotions.

## Optery
- **AI-powered removals via OpenAI now publicly documented** — positions Optery as the most technologically advanced consumer removal service; no other competitor has announced a comparable AI integration.
- Won "Market Disruptor – Digital Risk Protection" at RSAC 2026 (March 23, 2026); now holds 5 consecutive PCMag Editors' Choice awards (2022–2026).
- 2026 Enterprise Social Engineering Survey Report (released May 12, 2026) found: nearly all surveyed cybersecurity leaders (421 enterprise respondents) reported increased targeted social engineering; ~75% experienced credential compromise; data broker sites now ranked as the primary source of attacker intelligence, above social media and breach data.
- Enterprise push gaining traction: report amplified by a public-sector contract win and a planned webinar campaign — clear indicator of Optery moving upmarket beyond consumer.
- Platform covers 600+ automated broker sites; 1,000+ with custom requests; SOC 2 Type II certified.

## OneRep
- No new announcements in the last 48 hours; major news was the May 13, 2026 expansion to 550+ non-public data brokers.
- Custom removal requests and data breach monitoring (launched simultaneously with the May expansion) appear to be gaining traction in review coverage.
- Persistent reputational headwind: CEO's history founding people-search sites continues to surface in independent reviews and media comparisons, creating a trust gap vs. Incogni and Optery.

## Kanary
- **Published "Removaly: What Happened?" blog post** — formally addresses the shutdown of Removaly, a direct competitor. The post positions Kanary as a stable, long-running alternative and appears designed to capture Removaly's displaced customer base.
- Pricing: $16.99/month or ~$179.88/year; 14-day free trial continues to differentiate against Incogni (no free trial) and DeleteMe (scan-only free tier).
- 300+ broker coverage with continuous monitoring; 90%+ removal success rate within 14 days on people-search sites.
- US-focused; limited international addressable market vs. Incogni.

## Removaly
- Confirmed shut down. Kanary's blog post "Removaly: What Happened?" represents the industry's public acknowledgment of its exit. Previously covered ~50 sites; ProductHunt rating of 3.1/5; had following on Reddit for affordability.
- Customer base is now actively up for grabs — Kanary is the most visibly opportunistic.

## Regulatory & Policy
- **Vermont H 211:** Passed both chambers May 28–29, 2026; delivered to Governor Scott; had not been signed as of June 4. Key note: a provision for a single-portal mass opt-out mechanism was removed in the Senate — the final bill only mandates a feasibility study. Weaker than California's DROP platform.
- **California DROP + August 1 Mandate:** Starting August 1, 2026, California data brokers must comply with DELETE requests within 90 days. This deadline is approaching and may trigger last-minute compliance activity among brokers — a near-term market event to track.
- **JEC Report (February 2026):** Congress documented that data brokers cost American consumers approximately $21 billion annually — a figure gaining traction in press coverage and likely to support continued legislative momentum.

## Emerging / Noteworthy
- **PrivacyOn:** Newly appearing at the top of "best-of-2026" roundups with claims of 24/7 scanning and a feature set described as unmatched at its price point. No press release or funding announcement found — organic SEO traction or affiliate marketing play; warrants monitoring.
- **EasyOptOuts:** Continues to appear alongside Optery in Consumer Reports-based comparisons (65%+ profile removal rate); gaining mindshare in budget-conscious comparisons despite limited feature depth.
- **GhostVault:** Still positioned as the price/value disruptor ($39/year, 500+ brokers) in DeleteMe comparison content; no new announcements.
- **California DROP structural threat:** With 543 registered brokers now subject to a free, single-submission opt-out system, consumer-tier paid services face an accelerating "why pay?" objection in California — the largest US privacy market.

## Market Summary
Optery is widening its lead in enterprise credibility (RSAC award, AI integration, survey research), while Incogni's aggressive June discounting and family plan expansion are compressing margins in the consumer mid-market. The approaching August 1 California DROP compliance deadline and Removaly's confirmed exit are the two near-term events most likely to reshape competitive dynamics in the next 30 days.
