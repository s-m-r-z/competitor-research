## Market Summary
The single most important near-term forcing function remains California DROP enforcement on August 1, 2026 — with 260,000+ consumer deletion requests queued and $200-per-consumer-per-day penalties, CalPrivacy has now issued formal enforcement advisory FAQs signaling active scrutiny; the $1.5B single-cycle theoretical liability figure is entering mainstream tech press (TechTimes, July 8) and is a direct tailwind for every paid removal service's conversion messaging. The secondary story of the week is Aura's quiet emergence as a full-coverage competitor — its 200+ broker expansion and AI-native feature set are no longer niche, and its bundled distribution through an existing identity protection install base means it doesn't need to acquire customers through the same review-site channels that currently favor Incogni and Optery.
