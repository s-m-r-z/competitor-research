# Competitor Insights — 2026-06-23

## DeleteMe
- No new product announcements or pricing changes detected in the last 48 hours.
- Continues to differentiate on human-led removals vs. automation-first rivals; however a 2024 Consumer Reports investigation rated it a "mid-level performer" (below 65% profile removal rate), trailing Optery and Incogni.
- California's DROP platform (free, state-run competitor launching full enforcement Aug 1) is a growing headwind for DeleteMe's California subscriber base in particular — the service is now explicitly recommending itself primarily to non-California residents.
- A 2026 Surfshark review roundup continues to benchmark DeleteMe as solid but premium-priced relative to its documented success rate.

## Incogni (Surfshark)
- No new announcements in the last 48 hours; holding position as category leader by volume.
- Confirmed stats: 3,000+ unique domains in custom removal database (as of March 2026), 245M+ removal requests processed to date, 420+ brokers covered per independent Deloitte audit.

- Multiple June 2026 review roundups (Security.org, CyberNews, AllAboutCookies) continue to rank Incogni #1 overall; Editors' Choice from PCMag and PCWorld recognition sustained.

## Optery
- No new announcements in the last 48 hours; momentum from recent awards cycle continues.
- Pricing tiers as of June 2026: Core $3.99/mo (120+ sites), Extended $10.99/mo (370+ sites), Ultimate $24.99/mo (640+ sites + 955+ with custom requests) — most granular tier structure in the market.

- Y Combinator backing and enterprise positioning remain key differentiators; recent awards (3× Cybersecurity Excellence, Globee) bolster enterprise credibility.
- Optery's own published comparison page (optery.com/optery-vs-deleteme) is actively targeting DeleteMe's search traffic — aggressive content marketing posture noted.

## Privacy Bee

- Claims coverage of 1,000+ data brokers — the broadest stated coverage figure among tracked competitors, though independent verification is limited.
- Dashboard updates are weekly (better than quarterly competitors, worse than real-time). Differentiation via aggressive legal/regulatory pressure tactics when standard opt-outs fail.
- No new product launches or partnerships detected in the last 48 hours.

## Kanary
- **Strategic pivot confirmed**: Original web-based Kanary Cloud service is now closed to new signups — existing users only. All new customers are directed to **Kanary Copilot**, a mobile-first app.
- Kanary Copilot is now live on both **iOS App Store** and **Google Play** (Android waitlist previously; full availability now confirmed). Free tier scans 240+ broker sites and generates an exposure report in ~1 minute. Premium plan at $9.99/month.
- **Key limitation**: Copilot currently covers only ~10 websites for automated removal vs. 300+ on the legacy web platform and 300–640+ offered by Incogni/Optery/DeleteMe. A significant regression in coverage depth that could cost existing premium subscribers.
- Incogni published a direct "Kanary vs. Incogni" comparison post (May 30, 2026 via Geektown UK edition) exploiting Kanary's coverage reduction as a switching trigger.

## Emerging / Regulatory Developments
- **California DROP enforcement countdown**: August 1, 2026 is now 39 days away. After that date, data brokers must retrieve DROP requests every 45 days and complete deletions within 90 days or face $200/request/day fines. CalPrivacy has already levied enforcement: S&P Global fined $62,000 (313 days unregistered) and Datamasters (Rickenbacher Data LLC) fined $42,000 in January 2026. Expect escalating enforcement actions through summer.
- **US Senate data broker investigation**: Finding that Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, and 6sense Insights used code to block opt-out pages from search engine indexing is generating media attention and may trigger FTC referrals.
- **Congressional cost study**: A February 2026 CalMatters/Congress investigation found data brokers cost US consumers an estimated $21 billion annually — likely to fuel federal privacy legislation momentum in H2 2026.
- **BADBOOL (Big-Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List)**: Updated May 31, 2026 — community-maintained DIY alternative continues to grow, now adding Clustal and Men Stopping Violence opt-out instructions. Ongoing low-cost pressure on paid services.
- **DataPurge**: Emerging DIY tool generating legally-backed deletion letters citing CCPA, GDPR, and 17+ state laws; gaining SEO traction as a free alternative to paid removal services.

## Market Summary
The market's center of gravity is shifting toward the August 1 California DROP enforcement deadline, which will pressure data brokers to comply at scale and may commoditize California-market removal for paid services — watch for competitor repositioning toward non-California users or enterprise/B2B segments in the coming weeks. Kanary's pivot to a mobile app with dramatically reduced coverage (10 sites vs. 300+) opens a clear subscriber acquisition window for Incogni and Optery, both of whom are already publishing direct comparison content targeting Kanary users.
