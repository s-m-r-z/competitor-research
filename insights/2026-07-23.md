# Competitor Insights — 2026-07-23

## DeleteMe
- Continues operating at $10.75/month with a human-researcher + automation hybrid model, re-checking every 90 days across 122+ sites.
- A 2024 Consumer Reports study rated DeleteMe a "mid-level performer," trailing Optery and EasyOptOuts on removal success rates (below 65% of found profiles removed).
- As California's DROP enforcement deadline (August 1, 2026) approaches, DeleteMe's "we send deletion requests for you" value proposition faces pressure for CA residents who can soon submit a single request to 600+ brokers for free. The service will need to lean harder on monitoring, non-CA brokers, and enterprise positioning.

## Privacy Bee
- Won the "Best for Digital Footprint Removal" award in the 2026 All About Cookies Editors' Choice Data Removal Awards.
- Claims coverage of 1,000+ data brokers (400+ core, 1,000+ total including marketing lists) as of mid-February 2026, with weekly dashboard updates ranked by risk level.
- Positioned as the premium "always-on" option, emphasizing automation, family coverage, tracker blocking, and breach alerts — features that differentiate it from basic opt-out services as the DROP platform reduces barriers to entry.

## Incogni
- Current pricing: $7.99/month (Standard annual), $13.49/month (Unlimited), $14.39/month (Family), $20.69/month (Family Unlimited). All plans include a 30-day money-back guarantee.
- Covers 420+ brokers on standard plans, with 2,420+ on custom-request Unlimited plans. A Deloitte audit independently verified removal practices — a credibility differentiator no other major competitor has matched.
- Head-to-head vs. Optery: Cybernews and CyberInsider testing in July 2026 gives Incogni the edge on sustained backend suppression and non-public broker coverage; Optery wins on visual proof (before/after screenshots) and breadth of publicly visible listings.

## Optery
- Launched a free, open-source directory of 640+ data brokers on GitHub, complete with opt-out links, contact emails, step-by-step guides, and company profiles — a community-goodwill and SEO play that also undercuts manual-guide competitors.
- Released **Map Blurring** for Extended and Ultimate subscribers: users can request street-level imagery blurs on Google Maps and Apple Maps directly from their Optery dashboard — a novel privacy feature no other data removal service currently offers.
- Running a **Summer 2026 Sale** with 20% off using promo code `SUMMER2026`.
- Published a detailed explainer on how Optery functions as a California Authorized Agent under the DROP system, emphasizing that it provides immediate removals now rather than waiting for broker compliance on August 1.
- Current pricing: Core $3.99/month, Extended $10.99/month, Ultimate $24.99/month (all annual); Business/Family from $39/month.

## OneRep
- In May 2026, OneRep expanded coverage to include **550+ non-public data brokers** — behind-the-scenes databases used in marketing, HR, and profiling — available via its API and to individual users. This narrows the gap with Incogni's Unlimited plan.
- Ongoing reputational risk: OneRep's founder retains an ownership stake in Nuwber, one of the data brokers the service claims to protect against. No public resolution of this conflict of interest has been announced.
- **Mozilla cut ties** with OneRep, ending the Monitor Plus partnership. This removes a significant distribution channel and may dampen subscriber growth.

## Kanary
- Kanary has pivoted its product strategy: the service now centers on a **mobile app called Kanary Copilot** (iOS only; Android waitlist available), replacing the web-based workflow with bite-sized guided privacy tasks.
- This "task coach" model is a meaningful differentiation — Kanary is the only major competitor offering a mobile-first, guided experience rather than a fully automated background service.
- Pricing: $16.99/month or ~$179.88/year; 14-day free trial available. Covers 300+ brokers.
- The iOS-only status limits addressable market and may slow growth until Android support ships.

## Removaly
- **Effectively defunct.** Multiple 2026 sources confirm Removaly ceased active operations around early 2022. The service is no longer considered a viable competitor. Consumers are being redirected to DeleteMe, Incogni, Optery, or Privacy Bee as alternatives.

## Regulatory / Market Context
- **California DROP enforcement begins August 1, 2026**: Data brokers registered with the California Privacy Protection Agency must process deletion requests received via the DROP platform within 90 days or face $200/day per unprocessed request. With 600+ brokers in scope, this is the most significant regulatory event of the year for the industry.
- The DROP deadline is pressuring paid services to differentiate on value beyond simple request submission — continuous monitoring, non-California broker coverage, enterprise features, and verified proof of removal are becoming the key battlegrounds.
- A July 2026 head-to-head published by StateSurveillance.org comparing Optery vs. Incogni vs. DeleteMe is generating significant search traffic, suggesting strong consumer interest in comparative evaluations heading into the DROP deadline.

## Market Summary
The August 1 California DROP enforcement deadline is the dominant near-term force reshaping the data broker opt-out market: paid services are racing to articulate value beyond basic request submission, with Optery (open-source directory, Map Blurring, Authorized Agent positioning), Incogni (Deloitte audit, non-public broker depth), and Privacy Bee (premium automation, awards recognition) best positioned to weather the disruption. OneRep faces dual headwinds from reputational concerns and the Mozilla breakup, while Kanary's mobile-first pivot is a bold but risky strategic bet.
