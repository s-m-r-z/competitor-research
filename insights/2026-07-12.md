## Emerging & Market Signals
- **California DROP — T-minus 20 days:** August 1 enforcement begins; data brokers face $200/day/per-request fines. TechTimes (July 8) reported that at 260,000+ consumer submissions, non-compliance liability could theoretically exceed $1.5 billion in a single missed processing cycle. The consumer awareness spike this creates could be significant top-of-funnel for paid services — or undercut CA conversion with "why pay when the state does it free" narrative.
- **DROP adoption:** California's platform hit 242,000 sign-ups in its first 8 weeks of operation — ahead of projections; indicates genuine consumer demand for proactive data removal.
- **Senate investigation — dark pattern finding:** A 2026 US Senate investigation found that several major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) are placing code on their opt-out pages to prevent search engine indexing — a direct obstacle to self-service removal that strengthens the case for paid services.
- **Vermont DROP bill:** A proposed Vermont bill would create a second state-run universal deletion portal modeled on California's DROP architecture; multi-state regulatory pressure is building and will widen the market.
- **DataPurge** (emerging): Still appearing in 2026 roundups; legally-backed deletion requests citing CCPA, GDPR, and 17+ state laws sent in one click. No funding announcement detected yet — early-stage to watch.
- **DuckDuckGo Privacy Pro** (powered by former Removaly tech): Bundled in-browser VPN + data broker removal + identity theft remediation; unique distribution advantage via browser install base. Increasingly cited as an alternative to standalone removal services.
