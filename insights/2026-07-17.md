## Regulatory & Market Context
- **California DROP enforcement: T-15 days (August 1, 2026)** — 260,000+ California consumers have already submitted deletion requests (up from ~215,000 on July 16); non-compliance penalties of $200/consumer/day go live in 15 days
- **U.S. Senate investigation finding**: Multiple major data brokers (Comscore, IQVIA Digital, Telesign, 6sense Insights) placed code on their opt-out pages to prevent search engine indexing — actively obstructing consumer discovery of opt-out mechanisms; surfaces as a trust-and-urgency argument for managed removal services
- **Industry consolidation**: Publicis's 2026 acquisition of LiveRamp folds major consumer data infrastructure into a top-three ad conglomerate, alongside Omnicom's 2025 acquisition of Acxiom's parent; reduces broker ecosystem diversity and may increase consumer motivation to use removal services
- California DROP July 1 annual reporting deadline has now passed; data brokers were required to publish metrics on consumer requests received, fulfilled, denied, and response times — public data from compliant brokers will begin appearing in research
